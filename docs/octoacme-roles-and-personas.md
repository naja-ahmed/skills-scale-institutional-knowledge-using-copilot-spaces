# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers plan and oversee change initiatives within projects. They coordinate with Project Managers to ensure change impacts are understood and managed, while supporting teams through transitions and process improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and execute change management strategies
- Assess change impacts on teams, processes, and systems
- Coordinate change communications and training plans
- Track adoption metrics and resistance points
- Support stakeholder readiness and engagement activities
- Maintain change logs and documentation

### Goals
- Minimize disruption during transitions
- Maximize adoption and sustained change
- Ensure stakeholders are prepared and supported through change
- Build organizational change capability

### Typical Communication
- Change impact assessments and readiness reports
- Stakeholder engagement plans and status updates
- Training materials and communication campaigns
- Weekly coordination with Project Manager and Stakeholder Liaison

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Aligns on project timeline, coordinates change activities, escalates risks
- **Stakeholder Liaison**: Ensures external stakeholders are informed and prepared for changes
- **Team Members**: Provides support, training, and gathers feedback on change adoption
- **Product Manager**: Understands feature rollout plans and communicates user impact

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches guide teams in agile practices, facilitate continuous improvement, and help remove impediments to team effectiveness. They work across the organization to build agile capability and mindset.

### Responsibilities
- Coach teams on agile principles and practices (Scrum, Kanban, etc.)
- Facilitate retrospectives, planning sessions, and team workshops
- Identify and help remove organizational impediments
- Support team self-organization and decision-making
- Track and improve team health and velocity metrics
- Mentor Project Managers and team leads on agile approaches

### Goals
- Improve team effectiveness and delivery predictability
- Foster continuous learning and improvement culture
- Increase team autonomy and ownership
- Build sustainable pace and healthy team dynamics

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of ceremonies (standups, retrospectives, planning)
- One-on-one coaching sessions with team members
- Team health assessments and improvement roadmaps
- Community of practice sessions and knowledge sharing

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on delivery planning, shares team insights, coaches on facilitation
- **Product Owner**: Supports backlog refinement and stakeholder collaboration techniques
- **Team Members**: Provides coaching, facilitates team events, helps resolve conflicts
- **Developers**: Coaches on technical practices (TDD, pairing, continuous integration)

---

## Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons act as the primary point of contact for external stakeholders, ensuring their feedback is communicated to the project team and managing expectations throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Serve as single point of contact for stakeholder groups
- Gather, prioritize, and communicate stakeholder feedback
- Manage stakeholder expectations and engagement
- Coordinate stakeholder reviews and approvals
- Track and resolve stakeholder concerns and requests
- Maintain stakeholder communication plans and registers

### Goals
- Ensure stakeholder voices are heard and considered
- Maintain positive stakeholder relationships
- Reduce project team interruptions from ad-hoc stakeholder requests
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction with project outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Weekly stakeholder updates and feedback summaries
- Monthly stakeholder review sessions
- Escalation reports for critical issues
- Requirements clarification and decision documentation

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on timeline, provides stakeholder feedback, escalates concerns
- **Product Manager**: Translates stakeholder needs into product requirements and priorities
- **Change Manager**: Aligns on stakeholder readiness and communication plans
- **External Stakeholders**: Regular touchpoints, feedback collection, expectation management

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define quality standards, oversee testing strategies and execution, and work with developers to ensure deliverables meet acceptance criteria. They champion quality throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Design and oversee test strategies (unit, integration, E2E, performance)
- Lead bug triage and prioritization
- Coordinate testing activities and resources
- Implement and maintain test automation frameworks
- Report on quality metrics and release readiness

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality, reliable product releases
- Minimize production defects and rework
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Foster a quality-first culture across the team

### Typical Communication
- Daily bug triage and test status updates
- Weekly quality metrics and release readiness reports
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Quality retrospectives and improvement initiatives

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Aligns on quality gates, reports test progress, escalates quality risks
- **Developers**: Reviews code quality, pairs on test automation, coordinates bug fixes
- **Product Manager**: Validates acceptance criteria, prioritizes quality vs. speed trade-offs
- **Agile Coach**: Collaborates on improving team quality practices and definition of done

## Role Integration and Handoff Points

The expanded set of roles creates a more robust project delivery framework with clear handoff points and accountability. Below are key integration patterns:

### Project Initiation Phase
1. **Project Manager** creates project charter and coordinates team formation
2. **Stakeholder Liaison** identifies key stakeholders and establishes communication plans
3. **Change Manager** assesses organizational change impact and readiness
4. **Product Manager** defines vision and success metrics
5. **QA Lead** reviews acceptance criteria and defines quality standards

### Planning Phase
1. **Product Manager** and **Stakeholder Liaison** gather and prioritize requirements
2. **Developers** provide technical estimates and identify dependencies
3. **Agile Coach** facilitates planning sessions and helps establish team cadence
4. **QA Lead** develops test strategy aligned with delivery milestones
5. **Project Manager** synthesizes inputs into integrated project plan

### Execution Phase
1. **Developers** implement features following quality standards
2. **QA Lead** coordinates testing activities and bug triage
3. **Agile Coach** facilitates daily standups and removes impediments
4. **Project Manager** tracks progress and manages risks
5. **Change Manager** prepares stakeholders for upcoming changes
6. **Stakeholder Liaison** provides regular updates and gathers feedback

### Release Phase
1. **QA Lead** validates release readiness and signs off on quality gates
2. **Project Manager** coordinates release activities and communications
3. **Change Manager** executes change communications and training
4. **Stakeholder Liaison** manages stakeholder expectations and communicates release details
5. **Product Manager** validates business outcomes and success metrics

### Retrospective Phase
1. **Agile Coach** facilitates retrospective sessions
2. **Project Manager** captures learnings and action items
3. All roles participate in identifying improvements and celebrating successes

### Critical Handoff Checklists

**Requirements to Development Handoff:**
- [ ] Stakeholder Liaison has gathered and documented requirements
- [ ] Product Manager has prioritized and defined acceptance criteria
- [ ] QA Lead has reviewed and approved testability
- [ ] Project Manager has validated resource availability
- [ ] Agile Coach has facilitated backlog refinement

**Development to Testing Handoff:**
- [ ] Developers have completed code review and automated tests
- [ ] QA Lead has received deployment to test environment
- [ ] Test cases aligned with acceptance criteria are available
- [ ] Known issues and limitations are documented
- [ ] Definition of done checklist is completed

**Testing to Release Handoff:**
- [ ] QA Lead has signed off on quality gates
- [ ] Project Manager has validated release readiness
- [ ] Change Manager has completed stakeholder readiness activities
- [ ] Stakeholder Liaison has communicated release details
- [ ] Rollback plan is documented and validated
